Research

My research investigates how we are able to intake, interpret, and respond to speech rapidly and efficiently during everyday conversation. I explore how prior knowledge and other cognitive mechanisms facilitate spoken language processing. Additionally, I am interested in how people employ different strategies when the speech signal is degraded, from background noise or a hearing loss. Overall, I am fascinated by hearing science, cognitive science, and linguistics and hope that my work will ultimately inform communication strategies for individuals with hearing loss.
