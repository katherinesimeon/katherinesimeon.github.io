Katherine Simeon

Ph.D. Candidate · Northwestern University

I am a Ph.D. Candidate in Communication Sciences & Disorders at Northwestern University. I work in the Hearing & Language Lab, led by Dr. Tina Grieco-Calub. My research investigates how prior experience with sound and language impacts real-time language processing in children and individuals with hearing loss. Generally, I get excited about linguistics, cognitive science, phonetics, speech perception, education, and a lot of other things too.

Research

Research Interests

My research investigates how we are able to perceive, interpret, and respond to speech rapidly and efficiently during everyday conversation. I am interested in how prior experience with sound and language impacts spoken language processing–especially in children, who are still developing critical language skills. Additionally, I study how we employ different strategies in conversations when the speech signal is degraded, either due to background noise or a hearing loss. Ultimately, I hope that my work will inform communication strategies and educational practices for individuals with hearing loss.
